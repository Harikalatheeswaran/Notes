### 2-3. Full Workout Program
Warm-up (every session, 5-7 min): 3-5 min light cardio (cycle/elliptical level 4-5) + dynamic arm circles/leg swings + 1-2 light sets of first exercise.  

**Progression Rule (Progressive Overload):**  
Use weights allowing the top of the rep range with good form and 1-2 reps in reserve (RIR). When you hit the upper rep range for all sets (e.g., 3x12), increase weight by 2.5-5 kg (or smallest increment) next session. If form breaks, drop weight or add reps first. Track every session. Deload every 6-8 weeks (reduce volume/intensity 40-50% for 1 week).

Rest: 90-120s between sets (longer on compounds).

### 4. Cardio Strategy
Do **not** do elliptical every session (risks interference with lifting recovery/muscle preservation in recomp).  
Evidence: Both steady-state and HIIT aid fat loss equally when calories equated; HIIT is time-efficient and muscle-sparing but can fatigue CNS if overdone as a beginner.  

Prescription:  
- **2-3 sessions/week** of low-moderate steady-state (LISS): 20-30 min post-lifting or separate days (treadmill/cycle/elliptical at conversational pace, ~60-70% max HR).  
- **Optional 1x HIIT/week** (once strength base builds, week 4+): 15-20 min (e.g., 30s high effort/90s recovery on cycle).  
- Total weekly cardio: 60-90 min to create deficit without excess. Prioritize NEAT (daily steps ~8-10k) for belly fat reduction.

### 5. Strength Development Plan
**Pull-ups (0 → multiple reps):**  
- Weeks 1-4: Add negative pull-ups (jump to top, lower 3-5s) 3×3-5 + lat pulldowns/rows. Dead hangs 3×20-30s.  
- Weeks 5-8: Band-assisted or machine-assisted if available; aim for 1-3 full reps.  
- Ongoing: Grease-the-groove (submax sets throughout day) + consistent vertical pulling volume. Expect first strict pull-up in 8-12 weeks with progression; 5+ reps by month 6-7.  

**Push-ups (15-18 → 30+):**  
- Train push-up variations 2-3x/week outside main lifts (e.g., end of Upper days).  
- Progression: Standard push-ups → add pause at bottom or elevate feet for difficulty. Use clusters (e.g., 5-6 sets of 8-10 with short rest) or pyramid. When 3× max >25 easy, add weight (vest/backpack) or diamond variation. Aim 30+ continuous by month 4-6 via volume + strength base from presses.

### 6. Fat Loss Strategy
Fat loss requires consistent calorie deficit (energy in < out); no spot reduction for belly/love handles — overall fat loss + muscle gain improves appearance. Evidence: Mild deficit (300-500 kcal below maintenance) + high protein + RT preserves/builds muscle during recomp, especially in novices with higher body fat.  

Practical: Track weight weekly; aim 0.3-0.7 kg loss/week initially. Combine with training for recomposition (possible at your stage). Increase steps/NEAT first before aggressive cuts.
